## What is AP Biology?

AP Biology is a College Board AP course equivalent to a college-level introductory biology class. It covers 8 units focused on molecules, cells, genetics, evolution, and ecological systems. The exam tests scientific reasoning, data analysis, experimental design, and biological concepts through multiple-choice questions and free-response questions (FRQs).

These notes give you quick definitions, key concepts, and exam tips for every unit. For full guides with practice questions and flashcards, visit the live course on apscore5.com.

## The 4 Big Ideas

AP Biology is organized around 4 Big Ideas that connect every unit:

1. **Evolution** — life evolves over time
2. **Energetics** — biological systems use energy and molecular building blocks to grow, reproduce, and maintain stability
3. **Information storage and transmission** — living systems store, retrieve, and transmit information
4. **Systems interactions** — biological systems interact in complex ways

Every FRQ tests at least one Big Idea — knowing them helps you frame answers.

---

## The 6 Science Practices

The AP Bio exam tests these reasoning skills, not just memorization:

1. **Concept explanation** — describe biological concepts and processes
2. **Visual representations** — interpret and create diagrams, graphs, models
3. **Questions and methods** — design experiments and procedures
4. **Representing and describing data** — work with tables, graphs, charts
5. **Statistical tests and data analysis** — apply chi-square, mean, standard error
6. **Argumentation** — justify claims with evidence and scientific reasoning

About 50% of the exam tests practices 4–6 (data and argumentation).

## How to study AP Biology

1. **Don't just memorize — understand processes** — the exam rewards reasoning, not recall
2. **Master diagrams** — be able to draw and label cells, photosynthesis, respiration, meiosis
3. **Practice data interpretation** — every FRQ has a graph or data set
4. **Learn the formula sheet** — chi-square, Hardy-Weinberg, standard error appear often
5. **Connect units** — DNA (Unit 6) connects to evolution (Unit 7), energetics (Unit 3) connects to ecology (Unit 8)
6. **Practice FRQs weekly** — written reasoning is the hardest part of the exam

## Frequently asked

**How hard is the AP Biology exam?**
AP Biology is considered one of the harder AP science exams. About 65% of students pass with a 3 or higher, and around 17% earn a 5. The challenge is less about memorization and more about applying biology concepts to data and experimental scenarios.

**Is AP Biology harder than AP Chemistry?**
They test different skills. AP Chemistry is more math-heavy and quantitative. AP Biology has more vocabulary and concepts but emphasizes reading comprehension, data interpretation, and written reasoning. Most students find AP Bio's FRQs more challenging because they require longer written explanations.

**Do I need to take AP Biology before AP Chemistry?**
No. They have no prerequisite relationship. Most students take regular biology and chemistry before either AP version, but the order between AP Bio and AP Chem doesn't matter.

**What math is on the AP Biology exam?**
Basic algebra, statistics (chi-square test, standard error, standard deviation), and the Hardy-Weinberg equation. A formula sheet is provided. No calculus.

**How important is the lab component?**
Very. Many MCQs and FRQs reference experimental setups, controls, variables, and data analysis. If you skipped labs, practice with sample lab questions before the exam.
