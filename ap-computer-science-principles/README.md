## What is AP Computer Science Principles?

AP Computer Science Principles (AP CSP) is a College Board AP course that introduces students to the foundations of computer science — how data is represented, how algorithms work, how the internet functions, and how computing impacts society. The course is language-agnostic, meaning it focuses on programming concepts rather than one specific coding language. Students complete a Create Performance Task (a programming project) and take an end-of-year multiple-choice exam.

These notes give you quick definitions, key concepts, and exam tips for every unit. For full guides with practice questions and Create Task help, visit the live course on apscore5.com.

## Exam structure

AP CSP is unique — your final score combines two parts:

| Component | Format | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| **Create Performance Task** | Programming project (submitted before exam day) | 30% |
| **End-of-Course Exam** | 70 multiple-choice questions, 2 hours | 70% |

**No FRQs.** The exam is 100% multiple choice. About 57 questions are standard MCQs and 13 involve reading or analyzing code.

---

## Exam weighting by unit

| Unit | Topic | Exam weight |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Creative Development | 10–13% |
| 2 | Data | 17–22% |
| 3 | Algorithms and Programming | 30–35% |
| 4 | Computer Systems and Networks | 11–15% |
| 5 | Impact of Computing | 21–26% |

**Highest-weight units:** 3 (Algorithms & Programming) and 5 (Impact of Computing) together = over half the exam.
