## What is AP Human Geography?

AP Human Geography is a College Board AP course that studies how people interact with their environment, organize societies, and shape the world through population patterns, culture, politics, agriculture, cities, and economic development. The exam covers 7 units and includes multiple-choice questions and free-response questions (FRQs).

These notes give you quick definitions, key terms, and exam tips for every unit. For full guides with practice questions and flashcards, visit the live course on apscore5.com.

## Top 10 must-know models for the exam

1. **Demographic Transition Model (DTM)** — birth/death rates across development stages
2. **Rostow's Stages of Growth** — 5-stage economic development model
3. **Wallerstein's World Systems Theory** — core, semi-periphery, periphery
4. **Von Thünen Model** — agricultural land use rings around a market
5. **Weber's Least Cost Theory** — industrial location based on transport, labor, agglomeration
6. **Concentric Zone Model** — Burgess's model of urban land use
7. **Sector Model** — Hoyt's model of urban growth along transport corridors
8. **Multiple Nuclei Model** — Harris and Ullman's model of urban growth around multiple centers
9. **Central Place Theory** — Christaller's theory of city size and spacing
10. **Gravity Model** — predicts interaction between two places

## How to study AP Human Geography

1. **Learn the vocabulary first** — the exam is heavily vocab-driven
2. **Master the models** — at least one FRQ usually involves a model
3. **Practice with maps** — many MCQs include map stimuli
4. **Use real-world examples** — pick 2-3 examples per concept (e.g., a stage 2 country, a stage 4 country)
5. **Practice FRQs weekly** — graders look for specific terms and structure

## Frequently asked

**How hard is the AP Human Geography exam?**
AP Human Geography has one of the highest pass rates among AP exams, but scoring a 5 is still challenging. About 50% of students pass with a 3 or higher, and roughly 13% earn a 5. Strong vocabulary knowledge and FRQ practice are the biggest predictors of success.

**Is AP Human Geography a good first AP class?**
Yes. AP Human Geography is one of the most popular first AP courses because it has no prerequisites, focuses on critical thinking over memorization-heavy science, and introduces students to college-level reading and writing.

**What units have the most exam weight?**
Units 2 (Population & Migration), 5 (Agriculture), 6 (Cities), and 7 (Development) typically have the highest weight. Unit 1 is foundational but lighter on the exam itself.

**Do I need to memorize every model?**
You should know the major models listed above. The exam frequently asks you to apply a model to a real-world example or compare two models.
